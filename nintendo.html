About Nintendo

Nintendo has been at the forefront of video game innovation for decades, shaping the way generations have played, interacted, and imagined. Originally a playing card company founded in 1889, Nintendo entered the video game industry in the 1970s, but it was the launch of the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES) in the 1980s that truly revolutionized home gaming. The NES not only revived a collapsing gaming industry but introduced the world to now-iconic franchises like Super Mario Bros., The Legend of Zelda, and Metroid.

Nintendo's passion for innovation continued with the release of the GameBoy, a portable gaming system that defined handheld gaming and brought hits like Tetris and Pokémon into players' pockets. They expanded this with the Nintendo DS, which added touch controls and dual screens, and later the 3DS, introducing glasses-free 3D gameplay.

On the home console front, Nintendo consistently took creative risks. The Super Nintendo (SNES) brought enhanced graphics and storytelling. The Nintendo 64 introduced 3D worlds and the first analog stick, allowing players to explore games like Super Mario 64 and GoldenEye 007 with new freedom. The GameCube pushed visuals further and added multiplayer classics like Super Smash Bros. Melee.

In 2006, the Wii changed everything with motion-sensing gameplay that welcomed casual gamers and families into the fold. Titles like Wii Sports became household staples. Then came the Nintendo Switch in 2017 — a hybrid console merging handheld and TV gaming into one seamless experience, giving players the flexibility to game anywhere.

Nintendo Entertainment System (NES)

Released in 1983, the NES was a revolutionary 8-bit console that revived the home gaming market and introduced franchises like Mario and Zelda.

GameBoy

Launched in 1989, the GameBoy brought portable gaming to the world, bundled with Tetris and introducing millions to Pokémon.

Super Nintendo (SNES)

Released in the early 90s, the SNES offered improved graphics and sound, launching classics like Super Mario World and Donkey Kong Country.

Nintendo 64 (N64)

The N64, known for its groundbreaking 3D games, brought titles like Super Mario 64 and The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time to life.

GameBoy Advance (GBA)

The GBA offered powerful portable gaming with a huge library of colorful, fast-paced adventures and RPGs.

GameCube

Released in 2001, the GameCube introduced disc-based games and supported titles like Super Smash Bros. Melee and Metroid Prime.

Nintendo DS

With dual screens and a touchscreen, the DS changed portable gaming forever, with hits like Brain Age and Mario Kart DS.

Wii

The Wii brought motion control to the masses and became one of Nintendo’s best-selling consoles with games like Wii Sports and Mario Galaxy.

Nintendo 3DS

Adding glasses-free 3D to the DS line, the 3DS featured top-tier handheld graphics and beloved titles like Animal Crossing and Fire Emblem.

Nintendo Switch

Combining handheld and docked modes, the 2017 Switch is a hybrid console known for flexibility and top-tier games like Zelda: Breath of the Wild.

Nintendo Switch OLED

An upgraded Switch model with a stunning 7-inch OLED screen, improved audio, and better kickstand for handheld gaming.
