About Xbox

Xbox, launched by Microsoft in 2001, marked the company’s bold entry into the gaming industry. Designed to compete directly with Sony’s PlayStation and Nintendo’s consoles, the original Xbox introduced groundbreaking hardware capabilities, including an internal hard drive and built-in Ethernet for online gaming — setting a new standard for the industry.

Over the years, Xbox has evolved through multiple generations, from the original Xbox to the powerful Xbox 360, the innovative Xbox One, and the cutting-edge Xbox Series X|S. With each release, Microsoft has pushed the boundaries of what consoles could achieve, offering enhanced graphics, faster performance, and richer online experiences through services like Xbox Live and Xbox Game Pass.

Xbox has also become a symbol of community, creativity, and innovation in gaming, bringing players together across the globe. This page explores the remarkable journey of Xbox — from its ambitious beginnings to its current role as a leader in next-generation gaming, shaping the future of interactive entertainment worldwide.
